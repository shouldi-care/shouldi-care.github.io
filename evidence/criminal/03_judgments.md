# Document 03: Original Judgment - "Pleaded Guilty"

## Source
PACER, Document 377
USA v. Channon, 1:13-cr-966 (D.N.M.)
Filed: [October 2016]

---

## The Error

The original judgment entered by the court stated that Matthew Channon **"pleaded guilty"** to the charges.

This was false.

Matthew Channon went to trial. He was found guilty by a jury after a multi-day trial. He never entered a guilty plea.

---

## Significance

A judgment is the official record of a conviction. It determines:
- The basis for imprisonment
- The record that follows you for life
- The foundation for any appeal

A judgment that says "pleaded guilty" when the defendant went to trial is not a clerical error - it's a fundamentally different proceeding with different constitutional protections.

**A guilty plea waives:**
- Right to jury trial
- Right to confront witnesses
- Right to present a defense

**A jury trial preserves all of these rights.**

If the judgment says "pleaded guilty," it implies none of those trial rights were exercised - or needed to be protected.

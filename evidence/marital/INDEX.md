# Marital Epic: Cooperation, Coercion, and Coordinated Betrayal

## The Case in 60 Seconds

Matthew and Brandi Channon were co-defendants in a federal wire fraud case. Same charges. Same trial. Same jury. Same judge.

**Matthew got prison. Brandi got probation.**

The timeline suggests Brandi may have been cooperating with the government before, during, and after the prosecution - and that this cooperation included helping suppress Matthew's defense.

## The Pattern

### Before Prosecution
- Brandi gives sudden ultimatum after 6 years together
- Marriage occurs
- FBI raids 2.5 months later while Matthew is away

### During Prosecution
- Brandi recommends the first attorney
- Her attorney files 10 strategic unavailability notices
- Both defense attorneys attend meeting where exculpatory evidence is shown
- Neither attorney presents the evidence
- She never testifies at trial

### After Prosecution
- She gets probation while he gets prison
- She initiates divorce while he's incarcerated
- Divorce decree contains forged signatures and expired notary seal

## The Raid

When the FBI raided the Channon residence:
- Matthew was out of town on business
- Brandi was home
- She called Matthew from the landline
- She reported being pulled outside in her underwear
- Assault rifles pointed at her
- Neighbors watching
- Three houseguests present
- "FBI SQUAD 12" document listed 14 handwritten names

---

## Questions

1. **Why was Matthew conveniently away?** If Brandi knew the raid was coming, she could ensure he wasn't there.

2. **Why the theatrical production?** Underwear, assault rifles, neighbors - maximum trauma, maximum witness intimidation.

3. **What happened in the backyard?** She was allegedly "interrogated" where houseguests could see but not hear.

4. **Why marry 2.5 months before?** Spousal privilege. If she was already cooperating, marriage would complicate her testimony against him.

# Document 04: Attorney Coordination

On the evening of January 18, 2016, the following people met:
- Matthew Channon (defendant)
- John Robbenhaar (Matthew's attorney)
- Todd Hotchkiss (Brandi's attorney)
- FPD office tech

At this meeting, a posterboard with exculpatory evidence was shown. The evidence would have helped **both** defendants - it showed OfficeMax authorized the conduct.

The next morning:
- Robbenhaar refused to use the evidence
- Hotchkiss did not use the evidence
- Neither attorney ever presented it to the jury

**Both attorneys saw evidence that could free their clients. Neither used it.**
